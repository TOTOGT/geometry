# Book VI — Opening Note (draft)

## The "io, not oi" framing

Book 6 opens by stating plainly: this volume is a **natural continuation** of the
series into complex territory — and "complex" here does not mean "imaginary" in
the colloquial sense. It means $\mathbb{C}$, the field where $J^2 = -\mathrm{id}$,
derived in Book 4 from the commutator $[F,T]$.

The framing device: **io, not oi**.

- "Some Old Bullshit" (Beastie Boys, *Some Old Bullshit*, 1994) samples earlier
  punk-era material **backwards**. Run forward, a chant reads "oi oi oi" — the
  first-wave punk call-and-response. Reversed, it becomes **"io io io."**
- *io* is also $i$ and $o$ — the imaginary unit and the empty/zero, or the unit
  circle parametrization $e^{i\theta}$ traced as a loop (a "yoyo").
- The Beastie Boys' *Bodhisattva Vow* (also *Ill Communication*, 1994) — jazz,
  punk, hip-hop, Buddhist vow, from a NYC scene — is the tonal model for Book 6's
  voice: serious commitment (the vow / the proof) carried in a register that
  doesn't pretend to be solemn about it.

**The pedagogical point:** novice readers hit $i = \sqrt{-1}$ and reach for
"imaginary = not real = doesn't exist." Book 6 should open by dismantling that
before a single equation appears — using the io/oi reversal as the hook. Complex
numbers are not the *opposite* of real; they are real numbers plus a 90° rotation
operator. The "imaginary" axis is exactly as real as the rotation that takes you
from facing north to facing east.

## Structural note

This ties directly to Vol IV Ch 15 (*The Complex Turn*): $J = \tfrac{1}{\lambda}[F,T]$,
$J^2 = -\mathrm{id}$. Book 6 should open by re-deriving this informally in plain
language before the formal recap, using the io/rotation framing.

## The turn — "Sure Shot," not just "Bodhisattva Vow"

Album order matters. On *Ill Communication*, "Sure Shot" (track 2) is the early
turn — the line "I want to say a little something that's long overdue / the
disrespect to women has got to be through" — an explicit, unembarrassed
retraction of the earlier joke-era persona, delivered *before* the album earns
any spiritual weight. "Bodhisattva Vow" comes much later (track 13), as
something closer to a culmination.

The same two-beat structure should govern Book 6:

1. **The Sure Shot moment (early, Book 6 opening).** The io/oi wordplay, yoyo,
   "Some Old Bullshit" — all of that is real and stays. But Book 6 should
   contain one explicit turn, early, that says: the joke was the door, not the
   room. $J^2 = -\mathrm{id}$ is not a pun about "io." It is a derived fact
   from Ch 15's commutator. State this plainly, once, without irony, and move
   on. Don't apologize for the wordplay — just stop leaning on it as the
   argument.

2. **The Bodhisattva Vow moment (late — close of Vol VI or threshold of
   Vol VII).** The four-vow structure (zeros numberless / non-integrability
   inexhaustible / root systems boundless / Global Positivity unsurpassable —
   "I vow to attempt it") belongs at the *end*, after E₈, where the series
   has actually earned the register. Vowing to attempt the Global Positivity
   Theorem means more after 100+ pages of root-system machinery than it would
   as a cute epigraph on page 1.

**Net effect:** playfulness up front, an unforced turn to seriousness early in
Book 6 (the "Sure Shot"), and the vow held in reserve as the closing gesture of
the E₈ volume — earned, not decorative.
